The air tonight has that thick, warm summer weight to it, the kind that makes you just want to drive with the windows all the way down. We ended up at T4 for late-night boba—classic move. I got my usual passion fruit green tea, 50% sweet, while Marcus ordered some monstrous taro slush that looked like purple spackle. We dragged the metal chairs out onto the patio, scraping them obnoxiously against the concrete, and just sat there under the buzzing neon sign.

It is wild to look around the table right now and realize how soon everything changes. In just a few weeks, we are all scattering across the map. Sarah is heading out to NYU, Marcus is packing up for UT Austin, and I’ll be moving into my dorm at Berkeley. But tonight, none of that logistics stuff matters. We are just here, laughing until our ribs ache over some stupid joke Dave made about seagulls stealing his french fries at the boardwalk earlier today. 

The conversation bounces everywhere—from debating which late-night taco stand we should hit up next to arguing over who is going to have the worst dining hall food. There is this electric, almost chaotic energy in the air. Nobody wants to go home, and nobody wants this specific night to end. The ice in my cup is melting, watering down the last sip of tea, but I am in absolutely no rush to leave. We are just sitting here, soaking in the humidity, the streetlights flickering overhead, and the loud, overlapping voices of my best friends. It feels good just to exist right here, right now, completely present before the chaos of packing and moving actually starts.
