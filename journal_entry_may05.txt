Today I did something entirely out of character: I left my laptop at home. Right now, I'm sitting in the corner booth of the local coffee shop, nursing an iced Americano that's mostly melted ice at this point, and explicitly doing *nothing* productive. It feels weirdly revolutionary.

My phone is buried at the bottom of my bag on Do Not Disturb. No charts, no terminal, no server pings. Instead, I have a cracked, dog-eared paperback copy of *Dune*. There's something intensely satisfying about the tactile feel of turning actual paper pages instead of scrolling. The smell of roasted espresso beans and the quiet hum of the cafe are incredibly grounding.

Honestly, half the time I’m not even reading; I’m just people-watching. It’s fascinating to observe the ecosystem of a cafe when you aren't completely dialed into a screen. A few tables over, there’s a guy aggressively hammering away on his laptop keyboard, looking exactly how I usually look when I'm knee-deep in a project. Next to the window, two older women are having an animated debate about a dog. The barista is in a total flow state, rhythmically tamping espresso and steaming milk like a metronome.

It’s loud—the hiss of the espresso machine, the clatter of ceramic mugs, the overlapping hum of a dozen different conversations—but it’s a chaotic sort of white noise that actually helps me decompress. My brain is usually running at a hundred miles an hour, optimizing, calculating, building. Right now, it's just idle. I’m simply existing in this little pocket of time, soaking in the caffeine and the slow pacing of a sci-fi desert planet. I really need to do this more often. Just me, some spice melange, and absolutely zero notifications.
