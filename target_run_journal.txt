August 5th.

Target today was an absolute warzone. I seriously underestimated the sheer physical logistics of moving to Berkeley. I rolled in with a shopping list that felt roughly a mile long, naive enough to think I'd be in and out in an hour. Instead, I spent three hours fighting for my life in the home goods section. 

Every incoming college student within a fifty-mile radius must have had the exact same idea today. The aisles were completely choked with oversized red carts. I had to physically dodge entire families having full-scale meltdowns in the bedding aisle over whether to buy the gray or the navy blue Twin XL sheets. (I grabbed the gray ones from the bottom shelf while they were distracted). 

Trying to find specific items was like a scavenger hunt where half the prizes were already looted. Command strips? Completely wiped out in the hardware aisle, but randomly piled up next to the shower caddies. Speaking of shower caddies, I spent ten minutes staring at a wall of plastic mesh bags, deeply questioning how many bottles of soap I actually plan to carry down a dorm hallway every morning. 

The volume of physical stuff you need just to exist in a tiny shared room is staggering. Mattress toppers, a desk lamp, hangers, an ungodly amount of laundry detergent, a Brita pitcher—my cart was overflowing by the time I finally made it to the checkout line, which wrapped all the way back into the women's clothing section. 

My feet are killing me, and my car is currently packed to the ceiling with Target bags and cardboard boxes, but at least the bulk of the dorm essentials are secured. Now I just have to figure out how to actually fit all of this into a tiny room.
